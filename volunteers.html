Volunteer FAQs

What qualifications do I need to volunteer?

Qualifications vary by role. Field responders need relevant certifications, while technical roles require specific skills. Many positions are available for those with willingness to learn and help.

Are volunteers compensated?

While most positions are unpaid, we cover all deployment-related expenses including travel, accommodation, meals, and insurance for field volunteers.

How much time commitment is required?

Time commitments vary from a few hours per week for remote volunteers to full-time during deployments for field responders.

Do I need prior disaster experience?

While experience is beneficial, it's not always required. We provide comprehensive training for all volunteers based on their role and experience level.

Can I volunteer remotely?

Yes, we have many remote volunteering opportunities in areas like data analysis, coordination, communication, and technical support.

How quickly can I be deployed?

Trained and certified volunteers can be deployed within 48-72 hours of a disaster, depending on the role and location.
